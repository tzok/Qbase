About


Quadruplexes are characteristic tertiary structure motifs that form in nucleic acids. They participate in various biological processes and are promising therapeutic targets in many strategies of drug development.

Here, we present a database of tetrads, quadruplexes, and G4-helices found in PDB-deposited structures. We show their structure parameters, unique dot-bracket encoding, arc- and layer diagrams, and structure-based classifications (ONZ and the Webba da Silva geometric formalism). Dot-bracket and graphical representations correspond to the ONZ taxonomy based on the secondary structure topology. It allows a classification of canonical and non-canonical quadruplexes found in unimolecular structures.

A selection of statistics summarizes the analysis performed automatically on tetrads and quadruplexes by their structural parameters and two classifications, ONZ and Webba da Silva.
